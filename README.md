# MBA_BusinessAnalytics_Project

PROJECT TITLE : A STUDY ON IMPACT OF AI ON SUPPLY CHAIN EFFICIENCY, CONCERNING ROBERT BOSCH.

PROBLEM STATEMENT:

Supply chain inefficiencies can lead to increased operational costs, delays, and resource wastage at Robert Bosch. 
Traditional supply chain management methods may struggle to handle complex logistics and demand fluctuations effectively.
The integration of AI in supply chain operations is expected to improve efficiency, but its actual impact at Robert Bosch. remains unclear.
AI-driven automation and predictive analytics can optimize inventory management and reduce lead times, yet their implementation faces challenges. 
The adoption of AI may require significant investment, employee upskilling, and overcoming resistance to technological change. 
Evaluating the effectiveness of AI in reducing supply chain risks and enhancing decision-making is crucial for sustainable growth.
There is a need to explore AI-driven strategies to maximize operational efficiency and competitive advantage in  Robert Bosch.

Objective Of The Study:

To identify AI-driven technologies used in the supply chain at Robert Bosch.
To assess the role of AI in improving inventory management and demand forecasting.
To evaluate the effect of AI on reducing supply chain costs and lead times.
To examine the challenges of AI adoption in supply chain operations.
To recommend AI-driven strategies for enhancing supply chain efficiency

Research Methodology:
STATISTICAL TOOLS PROPOSED TO BE USED:
Sample Size - A total of 70 employees from Robert Bosch. have been selected as respondents for the study 

Statistical tools used:
Percentage analysis
Chi-Square
Correlation

Outcomes of the study:

A significant portion of respondents comes from younger professionals who are likely more adaptable to technological advancements like AI. 
The remaining respondents are spread across older age groups, contributing diverse perspectives but in smaller proportions.
The majority of responses were received from the Logistics and IT & AI Operations departments.
Most respondents fall within the 4–6 years and 7–10 years experience range.
The majority of respondents are graduates or diploma holders, indicating a well-qualified respondent base.
The responses are almost evenly split, indicating a balanced mix of trained and untrained individuals in the area.
Overall, the majority of respondents have a positive perception of AI adoption in supply chain operations.
The responses reflect a mixed perception, with a notable portion acknowledging its benefits, but also a significant number expressing disbelief.
The results indicate divided opinions, with a considerable number of respondents not perceiving a significant benefit from automation.
The majority of respondents view AI-based demand forecasting as a valuable tool for optimizing procurement.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the study aimed at analyzing the impact of AI on supply chain efficiency at Robert Bosch has provided valuable insights into the transformative role of AI technologies in optimizing operations. 
The primary objective of assessing AI's impact on supply chain efficiency has been met, revealing that AI has brought improvements in areas such as procurement cost efficiency, demand forecasting, and inventory management.
However, the study also highlighted key challenges faced by Robert Bosch, including high implementation costs, data privacy concerns, and employee resistance to technological change, which have hindered broader adoption and seamless integration.
Based on the findings, it is clear that while AI-driven technologies have proven beneficial in improving supply chain performance, there is still room for enhancement. 
To overcome the barriers to adoption, Robert Bosch should focus on reducing the high implementation costs by exploring scalable solutions and ensuring robust data security practices.
Additionally, extensive training programs are essential to help employees adapt to AI-driven processes. 
By addressing these challenges and implementing AI-driven strategies that optimize inventory management and reduce lead times,
Robert Bosch can further enhance its supply chain efficiency, ultimately driving cost savings and greater operational agility.
